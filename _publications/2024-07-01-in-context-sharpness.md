This work introduces in-context sharpness, a metric derived from the internal representations of large language models, as a reliable signal for detecting hallucinated content in generated text. We demonstrate that this metric correlates strongly with factual accuracy across tasks, and use it to develop effective hallucination mitigation strategies that preserve generation quality.
